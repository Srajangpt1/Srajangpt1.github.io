Project Description

Today, the internet has catapulted us into a state of global connectivity. Our mobiles, laptops have become extensions of us. We use the internet for almost everything, be it business, finance, education or even recreation (social media, gaming and so on). This rapid growth in the use of the Web has given rise to a rich global, linked cyber ecosystem - in terms of data from multiple sources being linked (information, knowledge, resources and people) as well as a large number of connected devices. While there are a lot of advantages to this, there is also a flip side. The ease of availability of data and devices that are fundamental to this globally linked cyber ecosystem has also created a problem in which this system is vulnerable and open to malicious attacks and exploits, putting its users at risk. This is clearly evident as observed from the multitude of malicious attacks and exploits carried out by attackers, targeting financial institutions and even the personal identity and details of the users. Our aim is to develop an linked application that analyses the semantic data associated with malware and vulnerabilities to generate actionable insights. These insights will provide the users with information as to the characteristics of the attacks and exploits commonly being carried out today; the types of attacks being carried out and the technologies being targeted by attackers while carrying out these attacks. The aim of this application is to use linked data technologies to perform statistical analysis on the enriched and readily available semantic data about vulnerabilities and develop a type of decision support system, thus providing the users with insights and key indicators, helping them to make decisions with regards to the efforts needed for vulnerability identification, mitigation and prevention
